Outside Groups

While outside groups have always played outsized role in American politics, their influence has increased dramatically since 2010, when, in Citizens United v. FEC, the United States Supreme Court held that the first amendment prohibted the government from prohibiting independent political expenditures - that is expenditures made by outside groups not coordinating their activities with a candidate.

Contributions to candidates and their committees are strictly regulated and capped at a relatively low level - in the 2015-2016 election cycle, an individual can give a maximum of $2,700 to any candidate committee, or a maximum of $5,000 to a traditional Political Action Committee (PAC). The aftermath of Citizens United, however was to see a proliferatio of "independent-expenditure-only Political Action Committees", also known as Super-PACs. Contributions to these groups are entirely uncapped, enabling the wealthiest Americans to have an outized voice in recent elections.
